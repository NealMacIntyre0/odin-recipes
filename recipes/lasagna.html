Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a wide, flat pasta noodle, usually baked in layers with sauce and cheese. Originating in Italy, lasagna is one of the oldest types of pasta. It is typically made with several layers of baked pasta sheets separated by fillings such as ragù (a meat-based sauce), vegetables, cheeses (which may include ricotta and parmesan), and seasonings such as garlic, oregano, and herbs.

This recipe will guide you through making a classic, hearty lasagna that's perfect for family dinners or potlucks.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Ground beef
  • Tomato sauce
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Mozzarella cheese, shredded
  • Parmesan cheese, grated
  • Egg
  • Dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper
  • Garlic
  • Onion

Steps

  1. Brown the ground beef with chopped onion and garlic. Drain any excess fat.
  2. Stir in the tomato sauce and oregano. Season with salt and pepper. Simmer for 15 minutes.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix the ricotta cheese with the egg and some grated Parmesan cheese.
  4. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  5. Spread a thin layer of sauce in the bottom of a baking dish.
  6. Arrange a layer of lasagna noodles over the sauce.
  7. Spread half of the ricotta mixture over the noodles, followed by half of the meat sauce and some shredded mozzarella.
  8. Repeat layers: noodles, remaining ricotta mixture, remaining meat sauce, and mozzarella.
  9. Sprinkle the top with the remaining Parmesan cheese.
  10. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until bubbly and the cheese is melted and lightly browned.
  11. Let stand for 10-15 minutes before serving.
